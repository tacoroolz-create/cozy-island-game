CHARACTER 28: Gizmo (Monster - Nerd)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

TURN 1 - Gizmo:
"Dreamer! You have arrived at the perfect moment. I was testing a theory: do rocks roll downhill faster if they are round, or if they are simply enthusiastic? What is your hypothesis?"

PLAYER CHOICES:
[1] "Roundness definitely helps."
[2] "Enthusiasm seems like the real engine."
[3] "I don't think rocks are enthusiastic."

---

TURN 2A (if player chose 1):
Gizmo: "Roundness! A solid hypothesis. Less friction, more momentum, fewer awkward corners. Round rocks are the athletes of the rock world."

PLAYER CHOICES:
[1] "Athletic rocks. I love that."
[2] "Aerodynamic rocks are superior."
[3] "Corners do slow things down."

---

TURN 2B (if player chose 2):
Gizmo: "Enthusiasm as engine. Brilliant. Perhaps the rock's internal desire to reach the bottom is the true force. Physics may not agree, but philosophy does."

PLAYER CHOICES:
[1] "Physics and philosophy can disagree."
[2] "A determined rock is unstoppable."
[3] "Maybe rocks have hidden motivation."

---

TURN 2C (if player chose 3):
Gizmo: "Not enthusiastic? How can you be sure? They don't speak, but silence is not absence of enthusiasm. Maybe they are simply very focused rocks."

PLAYER CHOICES:
[1] "Focused rocks is a good reframe."
[2] "Maybe they're just shy."
[3] "I'll try to see rocks as enthusiastic."

---

TURN 3AA (if player chose 1 then 1):
Gizmo: "Athletic rocks! They train by sitting still for centuries, then suddenly rolling with great purpose. Their training regimen is mostly patience."

---

TURN 3AB (if player chose 1 then 2):
Gizmo: "Superior aerodynamics. A flat rock would tumble chaotically. A round rock commits to its path. Commitment is underrated in geology."

---

TURN 3AC (if player chose 1 then 3):
Gizmo: "Corners slow everything. Trees, rocks, conversations. Corners catch on things. Roundness is nature's way of saying, 'Let's keep moving.'"

---

TURN 3BA (if player chose 2 then 1):
Gizmo: "They can disagree. Physics says gravity. Philosophy says will. Maybe a rolling rock is the meeting point between the two. A tiny conference of forces."

---

TURN 3BB (if player chose 2 then 2):
Gizmo: "Unstoppable! Once a rock decides to roll, who am I to argue? I would simply step aside and salute its commitment to descent."

---

TURN 3BC (if player chose 2 then 3):
Gizmo: "Hidden motivation. Exactly. They may dream of beaches, of ponds, of being skipped across water. Rocks have inner lives. Probably. We cannot prove otherwise."

---

TURN 3CA (if player chose 3 then 1):
Gizmo: "Good reframe. Focused rather than enthusiastic. A focused rock does not waste energy. It conserves, waits, then rolls with maximum efficiency."

---

TURN 3CB (if player chose 3 then 2):
Gizmo: "Shy rocks. That explains so much. They don't roll because they're show-offs. They roll because they got startled and are trying to leave gracefully."

---

TURN 3CC (if player chose 3 then 3):
Gizmo: "Try it. Next time you see a rock, think, 'That rock is enthusiastic about existing.' It changes the whole relationship. Rocks become friends."
