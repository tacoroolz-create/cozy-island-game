# Cool Valley

## Core
An autumn-equinox holiday inspired by Japanese Shūbun no Hi / Ohigan and Chinese moon-viewing traditions, reframed as a cozy island remembrance walk. Neighbors gather at dusk and walk to a high point to watch the moon rise, leaving small offerings at memory stones along the way. At the top, everyone shares sweet rice treats by lantern light.

## Existing Systems Used
- Existing island landmarks / special tiles
- Inventory / item system (flowers, harvestables, lanterns)
- NPC movement / dialogue
- Player interact (Enter / click)
- Day/night cycle

## New Items
- **Lantern** — temporary light source; can be crafted from sticks + fiber or reused from existing lantern/furniture items.
- **Sweet Rice Ball** — simple communal treat, possibly crafted from rice/wild grain + sweetener, or received from a neighbor.
- **Chrysanthemum** — optional seasonal flower that spawns during Cool season and can be left as an offering.

## Gameplay Flow
1. **Late afternoon / dusk:** Neighbors begin gathering at a starting point and mention the memory walk.
2. **Place lanterns:** The player lights and places lanterns along the path to the island’s high point.
3. **Memory stones:** Along the route are existing special spots (rocks, trees, old furniture). The player or neighbors can leave a flower or harvestable at each as a small offering.
4. **Moon rise:** At the high point, neighbors sit together and watch the moon appear. The time window is cozy, not strict.
5. **Sweet treats:** The player hands out Sweet Rice Balls. Each one shared gives a friendship boost.
6. **End of night:** Neighbors stay out a little later than usual, then disperse. Lanterns remain until morning.

## Constraints
- No actual death or ancestor worship; “memory stones” honor good island moments and old landmarks only.
- Tone is warm remembrance, never dark or mournful.
- Uses existing tiles as memory stones; no new persistent tile type.
- Lanterns may reuse existing light/furniture assets.
