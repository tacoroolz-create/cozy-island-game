# The Neighborhood Time Capsule

## Core
A visiting historian buries a small box in a central spot and invites neighbors to donate one item and one sentence. The player contributes any existing harvestable, and the historian writes down a mangled version of what they said. The box stays buried until the same holiday rolls around again next cycle, when everyone gathers to misremember what they put inside.

## Existing Systems Used
- Existing harvestable resources
- A central town/landmark spot
- NPC dialogue
- Player interact
- 6-day holiday cycle

## Gameplay Flow
- Day of the holiday: a historian NPC appears in a central spot with a small digging site.
- Player can donate one harvested item and choose a one-sentence "memory" from a short list.
- Each neighbor also "donates" (flavor-only; items are not real) and has one line about what they contributed.
- The historian thanks everyone and buries the box.
- Next cycle, when the holiday returns, the box is dug up.
- The historian reads a mangled list of contents, blending everyone's donations into nonsense.
- Neighbors argue cheerfully about whose mangled item is whose.

## Neighbor Interaction
Donation lines:
- **Chester:** "I put in a pebble that looks like regret."
- **Luna:** "A moonbeam. I trapped it in a jar. It escaped."
- **Hudson:** "A perfectly good sandwich. Future us will thank me."

Reveal lines:
- **Mimis:** "The list says someone donated their own shadow. That was probably me."

## Reward
- Friendship boost with the historian on both donation and reveal days.

## Constraints
- Box is a temporary object; no persistent buried-item tracking needed, just stored text strings.
- Uses existing harvestables.

## Optional Upgrade
- Player's chosen memory is stored and mangled specifically, so they hear their own contribution read back wrong.
