# Sweet Valley

## Core
A spring-equinox holiday honoring the Island God. Neighbors speak of the Island God's expected arrival and the need for a beach altar. The player gathers materials, builds the altar on the beach, and makes the first-harvest offering. Any offering gives a special message and sound; offering a Yam rewards a Candied Yam that ties into the later Marge the Miracle storyline.

## Existing Systems Used
- Beach/shore tiles
- Inventory / item system
- Harvestable crops
- Player interact (Enter / click)
- NPC dialogue hooks

## New Items
- **Wild Yam** — harvestable from wild zones (beach edges / forest edges). Year-round, with a spawn bump during Sweet season.
- **Yam** — grown by planting a Wild Yam on tilled soil. Matures into a harvestable Yam crop.
- **Candied Yam** — reward for offering a Yam at the Sweet Valley altar. Description: "A yam glazed in something that sparkles. It smells like a miracle."

## Gameplay Flow
1. **Morning:** Neighbors mention the Island God is expected and that the beach needs an altar.
2. **Build altar:** On the beach, the player places 10 sticks + 5 stones (or equivalent existing materials) to construct a temporary altar.
3. **Neighbors react:** Once built, nearby neighbors give thanks or offer silly encouragement.
4. **Make offering:** Interact with the altar while holding any harvestable. A special message and sound play; all neighbors gain a small friendship boost.
5. **Yam offering:** If the offering is a Yam, the player also receives a Candied Yam.
6. **Sunset:** The altar disappears at the end of the day.

## Constraints
- The Island God giant turtle does not physically appear for this holiday.
- No new tile type persists beyond the holiday; the altar is temporary.
- Candied Yam is flavor-only until the Marge the Miracle storyline is implemented.
- All offerings use existing harvestables; no special currency.
